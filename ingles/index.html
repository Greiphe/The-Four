Paper

Paper is made up of millions of lint that come from plants, which we call FIBERS. (you can do a simple experiment, tearing a sheet of paper and observing the edge you will notice the lint). There are several types of paper. It can vary in weight, thickness, among other things.
But it is its porous structure, similar to some rocks (such as pumice stone), which gives it special characteristics, differentiating it from cotton fabrics.
In Brazil, eucalyptus is the most used species, due to its rapid growth, reaching around 30 m in height in 7 years.
The pulp and paper industry has been increasing its relevance, and its energy consumption has gone from 5% of final industrial consumption in 1970 to 16% in 2020, with an average consumption growth of 5.4% pa (1970 to 2020).

The sector can be segmented into cellulose plants, paper and integrated plants, which produce pulp and paper – avoiding the energy demand for pulp drying. Pulp production is characterized by large-scale plants, with a production capacity of around 1 to 2 million tons per year, intensive in technology.
