Water and Pollution Reduction

Did you know that papermaking is one of the most polluting processes out there? Producing one ton of paper emits more than 1.5 tons of CO2 equivalent. In other words, using it less will help reduce the amount of harmful substances in the atmosphere.
By abolishing paper in your office, you are consequently helping to conserve water sources. Each ton of paper generates (waste volume): 18 kg of organic pollutants discarded in effluents; 88 kg of solid waste (hard to degrade, such as carbonate sludge, fiber waste and primary sludge) that can cause major impacts on the environment if not treated correctly. regarding the environmental impact, many people associate it with the extraction of wood, responsible for cellulose, the raw material for paper. However, there is an even more alarming fact in relation to the paper cycle. For every kilogram of paper produced, 540 liters of water are used. That is, if a company that consumes 600,000 sheets of paper per year prints the documents on both sides, it is possible to save 384,000 liters per year, equivalent to supplying 30 families in the same period. After extracting the cellulose from the trunks, the material goes through several stages of washing, cooking and pressurization, which corresponds to up to 100,000 liters of water. In addition, for the production of each sheet of A4 paper, around 10 liters of water are spent. But, more than the high volume used, the manufacture of paper harms the tributaries and streams surrounding the pulp extraction areas.
